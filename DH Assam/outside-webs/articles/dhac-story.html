The SIDRA Student Union successfully concluded the third edition of the Sidra Literature Festival (SLF) on December 25, 2025, transforming the campus into a vibrant celebration of literature, intellectual discourse, and Assamese cultural heritage over six memorable days.

A Festival of Literary Dialogue

SIDRA, the official student union of our campus, organized this premier literary event that brought together students, faculty, and distinguished guests from across the region. The festival served as a platform for meaningful literary exchange and cultural celebration, fostering an environment where ideas could flourish and connections could be made.

The event featured distinguished guests including Mr. Ashraful Ali (Advocate), Mr. Atwar Rahman (Principal, Janandeep Junior College), and Mr. Aminul Hoque (Principal, Albert Public School), who engaged students in thought-provoking discussions about literature, education, and cultural preservation.

Immersive Cultural Experiences

Beyond panel discussions, SLF 2025 offered visitors a rich tapestry of experiences. A multi-language book stall showcased literary works from regional and international authors, while an expo of traditional Assamese ornaments highlighted the exquisite craftsmanship of the region. The festival grounds buzzed with the energy of discovery as attendees explored various exhibits.

"The Sidra Literature Festival represents more than just literary discussions—it's a celebration of our identity, our stories, and our cultural legacy. Events like these are essential for nurturing the next generation of thinkers and creators who will carry forward our rich traditions while forging new paths."

Visitors explore cultural exhibits and food stalls at the festival grounds.

The culinary dimension of the festival was equally celebrated, with a dedicated food stall serving authentic Assamese Pithas—traditional rice cakes that are an integral part of Assamese culinary heritage. This allowed attendees to experience the region's culture through taste as well as sight and sound, creating a truly multisensory celebration.

Evening Cultural Performances

Each evening, the festival came alive with cultural programs that showcased the artistic talents of students and local performers. The lineup featured soulful renditions of Assamese folk music and a memorable tribute to the legendary Bhupendra Hazarika, with performances of his iconic Bhupendra Sangeet that resonated with audiences of all ages.

Artists perform on stage during an evening cultural program at SLF 2025.

The tribute to Bhupendra Hazarika was particularly poignant, celebrating the bard whose work has defined Assamese cultural identity for generations. Students performed his timeless compositions, connecting the younger generation with this important cultural legacy while infusing the performances with their own contemporary interpretations.

Looking Forward

SLF 2025 stood as a testament to SIDRA's commitment to fostering intellectual growth, cultural pride, and community engagement on campus. The success of this edition has set a high standard for future festivals, with plans already underway for SLF 2026, which promises to be even more expansive and inclusive.

The student union extends its heartfelt gratitude to all participants, volunteers, sponsors, and the college administration for their unwavering support in making this third edition a resounding success that will be remembered for years to come.

About SIDRA Student Union

SIDRA is the official student union of our campus, dedicated to organizing academic, cultural, and social events that enrich student life and promote intellectual growth. The union focuses on creating platforms for student expression, leadership development, and cultural preservation. Through events like the Sidra Literature Festival, SIDRA aims to bridge tradition with innovation, creating meaningful experiences that resonate with the entire campus community.
